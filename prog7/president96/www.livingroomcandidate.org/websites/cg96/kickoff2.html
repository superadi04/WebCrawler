October 3, 1996

CLINTON/GORE �96
KICKS OFF EFFORT TO TURN OUT JEWISH VOTERS

The Clinton/Gore campaign announced the formation of the "Clinton/Gore �96 Jewish Leadership Council," a nationwide group of community leaders and activists who will maximize Jewish voter education and turnout for Clinton/Gore �96 in their communities.

The Clinton/Gore �96 Jewish Leadership Council held a nationwide kick-off conference call on the evening of October 1, 1996 in 60 sites spanning 50 cities with First Lady Hillary Rodham Clinton; Nobel Laureate Elie Wiesel; Dan Glickman, who is the Secretary of Agriculture; and Joseph Lieberman, who is a Senator from Connecticut.

The First Lady praised the "Clinton/Gore �96 Jewish Leadership Council�s" effort in the call. "I want to thank all of you for being part of this Leadership Council, taking the responsibility in these last five weeks of reiterating the significance of this election, and the importance of re-electing President Clinton and Vice President Gore. There are few communities in our country that can match the Jewish community for concern about social issues, our social fabric, tolerance, and respect for each other," said Mrs. Clinton.

Elie Wiesel, Honorary Chair of the Clinton/Gore �96 Jewish Leadership Council, said, "As we come close to the 21st Century, we need leaders with imagination and social justice, leadership with moral inspiration. This President has been a steadfast ally and friend to the people of Israel."

The participants also heard from Dan Glickman who highlighted President Clinton's achievements including the fact that "the deficit has come down markedly under President Clinton." President Clinton also signed the Religious Freedom Restoration Act, the Brady Bill, the Assault Weapons Ban, and passed an economic agenda that has created 10.5 million new jobs and the lowest combined rate of unemployment, inflation, and mortgage rates since 1968. Joseph Lieberman added, "We all feel a sense of deep gratitude at the great job that President Clinton and Vice President Gore has done for America."
