PRESS RELEASE
September 6, 1996

ASIAN PACIFIC AMERICANS UNITE AT THE DEMOCRATIC NATIONAL CONVENTION

Asian Pacific Americans came to Chicago to unite with fellow Democrats and to celebrate President Clinton�s outstanding record of accomplishments. Asian Pacific American political appointees, opinion leaders, and 162 Asian Pacific American delegates took part in convention proceedings to organize and further increase their involvement in the general election. Members of the Asian Pacific American press also covered the convention and were hosted at a media roundtable where Alan Wheat, Deputy Campaign Manager and Director of Public Liaison for Clinton/Gore �96; Congressman Robert Matsui; and Sharon Singh, National Director of the Asian Pacific American Outreach Office at Clinton/Gore �96 discussed how Asian Pacific Americans are increasing support behind President Clinton.

During the convention, President Clinton -- on his train trip, "The 21st Century Express" -- focused on the core principles underlying his and Vice President Gore�s leadership agenda: opportunity, responsibility, and community. On Monday, August 26, the President announced new crime initiatives that would keep guns out of the hands of domestic violent offenders, outlaw cop killer bullets, provide "gun-free" school zones and promote tougher sentences for gun-toting drug dealers. On Tuesday, August 27, he announced his new "America Reads" education initiative designed to ensure that every child is literate by the end of third grade. On Wednesday, August 28, President Clinton announced a new environmental initiative to protect all communities from toxic pollution. In his acceptance speech, he presented a comprehensive vision and agenda for the 21st century.

"The Democratic convention was a sharp contrast to the Republican convention. We focused on substantive issues, not petty insults. We talked about a bridge to the future, not the past. And we talked about policies that help our families and children -- better and higher paying jobs, better health care coverage, and increased access to education," said Singh. "Asian Pacific Americans were proud to be part of this historic event. We are ready to work hard to re-elect President Bill Clinton and Vice President Al Gore"
