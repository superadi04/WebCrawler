PRESIDENT CLINTON�S CRIME INITIATIVES: MAKING A DIFFERENCE FOR LATINOS

WASHINGTON--When comparing the crime record between President Bill Clinton and Bob Dole, the President wins by a knockout. In just four years, President Clinton has done more to help reduce crime in the Latino community than Bob Dole has done in 35 years as a Washington politician.

Under President Clinton, violent crime rates have gone down each and every year. In 1995, murder dropped by 8%, and robbery dropped by 7%. In addition, violent criminals are serving longer prison sentences. The average prison sentence for fire-arm related and violent crimes was 74.5 months in 1992. In the first two years under President Clinton, the prison sentence for the same offenders was 88 months - more than a year longer.

In addition, President Clinton has:
* Passed legislation to put 100,000 new police on the street. Thus far, over 44,000 police officers have been funded, and 20,000 new police are on the streets.
* Passed the landmark Brady Bill after years of Republican stalling. This law has kept over 60,000 felons from getting guns.
* Passed the assault weapons ban

"We're moving in the right direction. The new police officers are supporting people in the community - they're making a difference. We have to let the President finish the job," said Ken Mireles, Clinton/Gore �96 Director of Hispanic Outreach.

Bob Dole, on the other hand, has:
* Voted against the Brady Bill & the assault weapons ban
* Voted to cut Safe & Drug Free Schools Program by nearly half
* Voted against the Violence Against Women Act
* Voted against 100,000 new police

"The record is clear, President Clinton is the real champion of Hispanic Americans when it comes to reducing crime. Bob Dole, meanwhile, is all talk," said Alan Wheat, Deputy Campaign Manager and Director of Public Liaison for Clinton/Gore �96. "The Brady Bill, the Assault Weapons Ban, and 100,000 new police on the street--President Clinton is making a difference for the Latino community," added Michael Carbone, Clinton/Gore �96 Executive Director of Latino Outreach.
