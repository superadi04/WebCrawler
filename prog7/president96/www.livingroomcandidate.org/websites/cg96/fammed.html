THE FAMILY AND MEDICAL LEAVE ACT:
A RECORD OF SUCCESS FOR ASIAN PACIFIC AMERICANS

President Clinton�s Family and Medical Leave Act continues to be a winner for Asian Pacific Americans. Thanks to the law, millions of workers have been able to take time off from work to care for a sick family member without fear of losing their jobs.

Since President Clinton signed the Family and Medical Leave Act, employees across America have taken over 12 million job-protected leaves. In addition, 67 million eligible employees can rely on the Family and Medical Leave Act if and when they need its protection.

Besides being family-friendly, the Family and Medical Leave Act has not created significant costs or burdens on businesses; it has even improved productivity at some companies. The vast majority of employers covered by the law experienced no or only slight increases in administrative costs. In fact, many employers have found the law beneficial, contributing to increased productivity and reduced turnover.

"Obviously President Clinton�s top priority is families," said Alan Wheat, Deputy Campaign Manager and Director of Public Liaison. "President Clinton values what Asian Pacific Americans have valued for generations — strengthening families and providing better opportunities for our children."

President Clinton has proposed expanding the Family and Medical Leave Act to allow working parents extra flexibility through an additional 24 hours of leave a year for parent-teacher conferences and preventive medical check-ups.

Bob Dole, on the other hand, is opposed to the law. He voted against it while in the Senate and is against expanding the law as outlined by President Clinton.

"Clearly, President Clinton has led the way in protecting our families and children. Parents do not have to fear losing their jobs if they have a family emergency and must tend to a child�s illness," said Sharon Singh, National Director for Asian Pacific American Outreach. "President Clinton understands that it is our basic duty to safeguard our families and children. Strong and healthy families are essential in building a solid economy for the 21st century."
