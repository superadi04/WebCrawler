President Clinton: Working Hard for New Hampshire
Greater Prosperity, Safer Communities, Stronger Families
President Clinton Has Delivered on His Promises—Making New Hampshire a Better Place to Live and Work.   President Clinton has been a leader on the issues the people of New Hampshire care about. President Clinton has delivered:

A Growing Economy: Unemployment in New Hampshire has dropped: 7.6% to 3.8%. Our nation now has the lowest combined rate of unemployment, inflation, and mortgage rates since 1968. Consumer confidence is at its highest level in six years, real wages are rising for the first time in a decade, and homeownership is at a 15-year high.
New Jobs, Better Wages: 54,600 new jobs created under President Clinton after losing jobs during the previous four years; 10.5 million jobs created nationally. Because President Clinton signed the minimum wage increase on 8-20-96, 12,000 workers in New Hampshire received a pay raise. Over the next two years an additional 30,000 workers in New Hampshire will benefit from the minimum wage increase signed by President Clinton.
Stronger Families: The Family and Medical Leave Act protects 181,000 workers in New Hampshire—allowing them to take 12 weeks of unpaid leave for the birth of a child or to take care of a sick family member without fear of losing their jobs. Teen pregnancy is falling, the poverty rate is decreasing, the number of people on welfare is declining. In New Hampshire alone there are 5,172 fewer people on welfare and 3,202 fewer people on food stamps. Tougher child support enforcement collects nearly $15 million more -- an increase of over 55% since FY 1992.
Increased Access to Education: 109,700 students in New Hampshire can benefit from the President’s reformed student loan program. 1,232 students benefited from Head Start funding and New Hampshire will receive over $2 million in funding for the Goals 2000 academic achievement program.
Cut the Deficit in Half: Reducing the burden of national debt for every New Hampshire family of four by $15,000.
A Tax Cut: By expanding the Earned Income Tax Credit, the President cut taxes for 40,800 working families in New Hampshire and 15 million working families nationwide. President Clinton provided over 7,247 New Hampshire small businesses with a tax cut this year by expanding the annual expensing allowance from $10,000 to $17,500.
Safer Streets and Communities: The President’s Crime Bill adds 150 police officers to New Hampshire’s streets.  “Three Strikes and You’re Out,” the Brady Bill, the Assault Weapons Ban, and community policing are working. Our nation’s crime rate is down. The number of murders reported has dropped 8%—one of the largest declines in three decades. In New Hampshire alone the crime rate dropped 13.80% in the first two years in the Clinton Administration. In addition, New Hampshire received over $896,000 in Services, Training, Officers and Prosecutors (STOP) grants to combat domestic violence and sexual assault.
Pension and Health Care Coverage: Because President Clinton signed the Retirement Savings and Security Act, 38,000 workers in New Hampshire for small businesses will be offered expanded opportunity for pension coverage. In New Hampshire, as many as 102,000 citizens in families with a worker who changed, lost or left a job with health insurance will be guaranteed continued access to health insurance under the Kassebaum-Kennedy Health Insurance Bill which President Clinton fought for and signed into law.
Renewed Growth in Key Industries: After a decade of enormous job losses in construction, manufacturing, and autos, these industries have made a remarkable recovery—more than one million new jobs created under this Administration.

Fighting for a Balanced Budget That Reflects Our Values: Now we are fighting for a balanced budget that will lift the burden of debt off our children and reflect American values—a balanced budget that:

Honors our commitment to give America’s seniors the health care they deserve.
Protects investment in our young people’s futures—Head Start, access to college.
Protects America’s clean water and air, national parks, and our public health.
Preserves tax fairness for America’s working families.
