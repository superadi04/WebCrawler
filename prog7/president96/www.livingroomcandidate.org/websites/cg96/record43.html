President Clinton: Working Hard for New Mexico
Greater Prosperity, Safer Communities, Stronger Families
President Clinton Has Delivered on His Promises—Making New Mexico a Better Place to Live and Work.   President Clinton has been a leader on the issues the people of New Mexico care about. President Clinton has delivered:

A Growing Economy: Unemployment in New Mexico has dropped: 7.5% to 7.1%. Our nation now has the lowest combined rate of unemployment, inflation, and mortgage rates since 1968. Consumer confidence is at its highest level in six years, real wages are rising for the first time in a decade, and homeownership is at a 15-year high.
New Jobs, Better Wages: 108,000 new jobs created in New Mexico in 42 months under President Clinton more than during the previous four years; 10.5 million jobs created nationally. Because President Clinton signed the minimum wage increase, 35,000 workers in New Mexico received a pay raise. Over the next two years 62,000 workers in New Mexico will benefit from the minimum wage increase.
Stronger Families: The Family and Medical Leave Act protects 176,000 workers in New Mexico—allowing them to take 12 weeks of unpaid leave for the birth of a child or to take care of a sick family member without fear of losing their jobs. Teen pregnancy is falling, the poverty rate is decreasing, the number of people on welfare is declining. In New Mexico alone 5,744 fewer people are on welfare and 4,812 fewer are on food stamps. Tougher child support enforcement collects nearly $8 million more -- an increase of 41% since FY 1992.
Increased Access to Education: 75,800 students in New Mexico can benefit from the President’s reformed student loan program. 6,821 students benefited from Head Start funding and New Mexico will receive over $6 million in funding for the Goals 2000 academic achievement program.
Cut the Deficit in Half: Reducing the burden of national debt for every New Mexico family of four by $15,000.
A Tax Cut: By expanding the Earned Income Tax Credit, the President cut taxes for nearly 134,000 working families in New Mexico and 15 million working families nationwide. President Clinton provided over 8,405 New Mexico small businesses with a tax cut this year by expanding the annual expensing allowance from $10,000 to $17,500.
Safer Streets and Communities: The President’s Crime Bill adds 316 police officers to New Mexico’s streets and over $1 million in funding to combat domestic violence and sexual assault.  “Three Strikes and You’re Out,” the Brady Bill, the Assault Weapons Ban, and community policing are working. Our nation’s crime rate is down. The number of murders reported has dropped 8%—one of the largest declines in three decades. In New Mexico alone the crime rate has dropped and the state received over $1 million to combat domestic violence and sexual assault.
Pension and Health Care Coverage: Because President Clinton signed the Retirement Savings and Security Act, 58,000 workers in New Mexico for small businesses will be offered expanded opportunity for pension coverage. In New Mexico, as many as 85,000 citizens in families with a worker who changed, lost or left a job with health insurance will be guaranteed continued access to health insurance under the Kassebaum-Kennedy Health Insurance Bill which President Clinton fought for and signed into law.
Renewed Growth in Key Industries: After a decade of enormous job losses in construction, manufacturing, and autos, these industries have made a remarkable recovery—more than one million new jobs created under this Administration.

Fighting for a Balanced Budget That Reflects Our Values: Now we are fighting for a balanced budget that will lift the burden of debt off our children and reflect American values—a balanced budget that:

Honors our commitment to give America’s seniors the health care they deserve.
Protects investment in our young people’s futures—Head Start, access to college.
Protects America’s clean water and air, national parks, and our public health.
Preserves tax fairness for America’s working families.
