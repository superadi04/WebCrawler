For Immediate Release
October 10, 1996
PRESIDENT CLINTON SIGNS VETERANS LEGISLATION

Washington, D.C. -- President Clinton yesterday signed three pieces of veterans legislation that will ensure that veterans� benefits keep pace with inflation, improve the VA health care system, and expand numerous other veterans� programs and benefits.

"These three laws demonstrate again that the President has veterans� best interests at heart," said Tom Keefe, Director of Veterans Outreach. "From increasing and expanding veterans� benefits to improving VA care, President Clinton has been veterans� best friend in the White House since Franklin Roosevelt."

The first law, the Veterans� Health Care Eligibility Reform Act of 1996 (H.R. 3118), revises the archaic and arcane rules governing eligibility for VA care and gives the VA new tools to improve its efficiency. The legislation includes many proposals recommended by the National Performance Review headed by Vice President Al Gore in order to establish a modern, integrated health care system. Eligibility reform has also been a high priority of veterans� groups.

The second law, the Veterans� Compensation Cost-of-Living Adjustment Act of 1996 (H.R. 3458), authorizes a cost-of-living adjustment (COLA) for 2.3 million disabled veterans who receive disability compensation; over 300,000 individuals who receive benefits as surviving spouses or children of service members whose death was service-connected; and veterans who receive clothing allowances because they wear or use prosthetic or orthopedic devices.

The final law, the Veterans� Benefits Improvements Act of 1996 (S. 1711) expands and extends numerous veterans education, housing, life insurance, and employment and training benefits.

"Add these three new laws up and you get a man who looks after the needs of America�s veterans," said Deputy Campaign Manager Alan Wheat. "President Clinton has proven his commitment to veteran issues."
