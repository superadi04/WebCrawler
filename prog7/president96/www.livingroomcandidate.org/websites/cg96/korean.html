KOREAN AMERICANS FOR CLINTON/GORE�96

New York -- Tri-State area Korean Americans announced the formation of "Korean Americans for Clinton/Gore�96," a new national group in support of the reelection of President Bill Clinton and Vice President Al Gore in New York. The announcement was made on August 1, 1996 at the Macedonia Church in Flushing, New York with approximately 200 key Korean American supporters in attendance.

Special guest speakers included Sharon Singh, Assistant Director of Public Liaison for Asian Pacific American Outreach, Clinton/Gore�96; and Il-Pyong Kim, Professor of Political Science at the University of Connecticut.

"President Clinton is very much aware of the contributions that Korean Americans have made to society. His commitment to diversity and equality is evident in his Administration and policies," said Dr. Kim, who addressed the audience in the Korean language. Dr. Kim�s remarks centered on why Korean Americans need to support President Clinton in the upcoming election.

President Clinton and Vice President Gore have taken the lead on issues important to many of the ethnic communities including the Korean American community. Some of these accomplishments include:

  • Increasing access to college by reforming student loans and expanding work study;
  • Expanding trade markets in Asia and the Pacific for U.S. exports;
  • Appointing 195 political appointees to this Administration;
  • Signing the Family and Medical Leave Act so workers can take time off to care for a sick family member without losing their jobs;
  • Establishing Americorps so young people now have the opportunity to serve their communities while earning money for college.

Also attending was David K. Chai, Assistant to the Deputy Campaign Manager and Chief Operating Officer for Clinton/Gore�96; and APA Steering Committee members Seung Hong, Daok Lee, Jae Sung, and John Kim.

"The Korean American community is an integral part of the Asian Pacific American community. Their support for Clinton/Gore�96 is vital to the success of this national election. Korean Americans for Clinton/Gore�96 is open to all individuals regardless of party affiliation. The time has come for all Korean Americans to fully participate in the American electoral process," said Singh.
