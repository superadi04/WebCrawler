For Immediate Release
September 26, 1996
Prominent Veterans' Leaders Across the Nation Endorse Re-election of President Clinton and Vice President Gore

WASHINGTON — Prominent veterans� leaders and American heroes from past conflicts today declared their full support for the re-election of President Bill Clinton and Vice President Al Gore. At a news conference in Washington, D.C. and in press activities in 33 states, they announced the creation of the "National Veterans for Clinton/Gore Steering Committee."

In Washington, Jesse Brown, who is the Secretary of Veterans Affairs and a honorary co-chair of the Committee, stated, "President Clinton has done more to help our nation's veterans than any President since FDR signed the G.I. Bill. Veterans everywhere should know that the President has kept his promise to veterans with deeds not words to honor the service and sacrifice of every man and woman in uniform." Hershel Gober who is the Deputy Secretary of Veterans Affairs and a honorary co-chair, added, "We intend to make certain that the Republicans do not distort or misrepresent that record."

In addition to Jesse Brown, other prominent members on the national steering committee, all of whom have impeccable military credentials, include: Admiral Elmo Russell Zumwalt, Jr. (ret.), former Chief of Naval Operations and a member of the Joint Chiefs of Staff from 1970 to 1974; William C. Ferguson, a member of the Tuskegee Airmen; Brigadier General Pat Foote (ret.), former Commanding General of Fort Belvoir and one of the highest-ranking females to serve in the military; and Lt. General Thomas W. Kelly (ret.), former Director of Operations for the Joint Chiefs of Staff. (Attached is a full list of steering committee members).

In addition to the national committee, state advisory committees are being established to rally veterans in every state behind the Clinton/Gore ticket. The state advisory committees will be especially active in California, New York, Pennsylvania, Florida, and Michigan — where there are large veterans populations. The committees� will educate veterans everywhere about the President�s initiatives, conduct grass roots activities, and encourage veterans to vote for the President and a Democratic Congress on November 5.

Mr. James Dean, former National Commander of the American Legion, stated "I have been very impressed with the number of veterans contacting me in the past months indicating their strong support for re-electing the President and Vice President. We are looking forward to their victory in November."

Veterans for Clinton/Gore '96 National Steering Committee
Honorary Co-Chairs

Hon. Jesse Brown, Chicago, Illinois
Jesse Brown is the Secretary of Veterans Affairs. An ex-Marine, he was wounded in combat in Vietnam in 1965. He was the executive director of the Disabled American Veterans from 1989 to 1993. Secretary Brown was a featured speaker at the Democratic National Convention in Chicago.

Hon. Hershel Gober, Little Rock, Arkansas
Hershel Gober is the Deputy Secretary of Veterans Affairs. He is a close personal friend of President Clinton and served as Arkansas Director of Veterans Affairs while the President was governor there. He served for 20 years in the Marine Corps and Army, including two tours of duty in Vietnam, and has received numerous decorations including the Purple Heart, Bronze Star, and the Soldier�s Medal.

Honorary Vice Chairs

Senator John H. Glenn, Columbus, Ohio
For his service in 149 missions in World War II and the Korean conflict, Senator Glenn received many honors, including the Distinguished Flying Cross (six occasions) and the Air Medal with eighteen clusters. On February 20, 1962, he made history as the first American to orbit the earth, for which he received the Space Congressional Medal of Honor. He has served in the Senate since 1974.

Senator Daniel K. Inouye, Honolulu, Hawaii
A war hero, Senator Inouye was awarded a Distinguished Service Cross, Bronze Star, Purple Heart with cluster, and 12 other medals and citations for his service in France and Italy in World War II. He is now a nationally known leader and the fifth most senior member of the U.S. Senate who plays an important role in defense policies through his membership on the Appropriations Subcommittee on Defense.

Senator J. Robert Kerrey, Omaha, Nebraska
Senator Bob Kerrey earned a Medal of Honor for his service in the elite Navy SEAL Team, the Navy�s version of the Green Berets. Since his election to the U.S. Senate in 1988, he has impressed political observers with his independence, candor, and tireless determination to make government work.

Senator John Kerry, Boston, Massachusetts
Senator John Kerry served in the Navy in Vietnam, during which he received a Silver Star, two Bronze Stars, and three Purple Hearts. Upon his return home, he became an active leader of the Vietnam Veterans Against the War and a co-founder of the Vietnam Veterans of America. He has served in the U.S. Senate since 1984.

Senator Frank Lautenberg, Paterson, New Jersey
Senator Frank Lautenberg enlisted and served in the Army Signal Corps in Europe during World War II. Following the war, he went to Columbia University on the G.I. Bill and received an undergraduate degree in economics. He was elected to the United States Senate in 1982 and serves on the Appropriations, Budget, Environment and Public Works, and Small Business Committees.

Senator Charles Robb, McLean, Virginia
Senator Chuck Robb commanded an infantry company in combat in Vietnam and was awarded the Bronze Star with Combat V and the Vietnamese Cross of Gallantry with Silver Star. He was elected to the U.S. Senate in 1988, where he serves on the Senate Armed Services Committee, the Foreign Relations Committee, and the Intelligence Committee -- the only member of Congress in history to serve on all three national security committees. He spent 34 years in active and reserve duty in the Marine Corps.

Representative Lane Evans, Rock Island, Illinois
Representative Lane Evans served in the Marine Corps from 1969 until 1971. Since being elected the Congress in 1982, he has been recognized as a leading veterans advocate on Capitol Hill. Congressman Evans is a member of the House Veterans Affairs Committee and is ranking member of the Subcommittee on Compensation, Pension, Insurance, and Memorial Affairs.

Representative Sam Gibbons, Tampa Bay, Florida
Representative Gibbons was awarded the Bronze Star after parachuting into Normandy on D-Day as a part of the initial assault force during World War II. He was a captain in the 501st Parachute Infantry/101st Airborne Division. His career in public service began in 1952 with his election to the Florida House of Representatives. In 1962, he was elected to Congress where he has served as a member of the Ways and Means Committee since 1969 and Ranking Democratic Member in the 104th Congress.

Representative John Murtha, Johnstown, Pennsylvania
Representative Murtha served in the Marines after high school, volunteered for another tour of duty and went to Vietnam in 1966-67, earning two Purple Hearts, the Bronze Star and Vietnamese Cross of Gallantry. He received the Distinguished Service Medal of the Marine Corps when he retired from the Reserves. He was elected to the Pennsylvania House in 1969 and to Congress in 1974. He has served as Chairman and now Ranking Democractic Member of the Appropriations Committee and the Ranking Democrat on the Subcommittee on National Security.

Representative Frank Tejeda, San Antonio, Texas
After volunteering to serve in the Marine Corps in Vietnam, Congressman Frank Tejeda earned the Bronze Star and the Purple Heart. He holds graduate degrees from the University of California at Berkeley School of Law, Harvard University�s Kennedy School of Government, and the Yale University School of Law. He has served in the U.S. House of Representatives since 1993.

Lucien Adams, San Antonio, Texas
Lucien Adams was awarded the Medal of Honor for his service in World War II after single-handedly storming and knocking out three machine gun encampments that were interrupting American supply lines. He also received the Bronze Star and Purple Heart. He worked for the Veterans Administration for forty years and now serves on the staff of Congressman Frank Tejada.

Nathan Green Gordon, Morrilton, Arkansas
Nathan Gordon served in the Naval Air Corps from 1941 until 1945. He was awarded the Congressional Medal of Honor, two Distinguished Flying Crosses, and six Air Medals and is a member of the Naval Aviation Hall of Fame. He was Lieutenant Governor of Arkansas from 1946 until 1967, and then practiced law in his hometown, Morrilton, Arkansas, until 1987.

Hershey Miyamura, Gallup, New Mexico
Hershey Miyamura served in the U.S. Army and Army Reserve from 1944 until 1953. As a machine gun squad leader in the Korean conflict, he was captured by the Chinese Army and spent over two years in prisoner of war camp. Upon his return to the United States in 1953, he was awarded the Congressional Medal of Honor.

Ted Connell, Killeen, Texas
Ted Connell was the National Commander-in-Chief of the Veterans of Foreign Wars of the United States in 1960-61. During World War II, he served in the U.S. Army Field Artillery in the South Pacific Theater and is currently a Lieutenant Colonel in the U.S. Army Reserves. He was also twice elected Mayor of Killeen, Texas, now a city of 60,000.

James P. Dean, Corinth, Mississippi
James Dean is a prominent attorney in Corinth, Mississippi. He was the national commander of the American Legion from 1986-87. Dean served with the U.S. Army Security Agency during the Korean conflict and served eight years in the Mississippi House of Representatives and Senate.

William M. Detweiler, New Orleans, Louisiana
Bill Detweiler was the National Commander of the American Legion in 1994-95. He served in the U.S. Army at the U.S. Air Defense Command at Ft. Bliss in Texas, and later reached the rank of Captain in the U.S. Army Reserves.

William C. Ferguson, Sr., Cleveland, Ohio
William Ferguson served in the United States Army from 1942 to 1945. He is a member of the National Board of Directors of the Tuskegee Airman and the author of Black Flyers in WWII.

Mack G. Fleming, Seneca, South Carolina
From 1956 to 1958, Mack Fleming served in the U.S. Army with the Second Armored Division in Europe. Until his retirement in 1995, he had been the Democratic chief counsel for the U.S. House of Representatives Committee on Veterans Affairs.

Evelyn P. Foote, Accokeek, Maryland
Brigadier General Pat Foote served in the U.S. Army from 1960 until 1989, where she worked her way up to an assignment as Commanding General of Fort Belvoir, Virginia. Her awards include the Distinguished Service Medal, the Legion of Merit (with Oak Leaf Cluster), and the Bronze Star Medal. She is the Commissioner of the American Battle Monuments Commission. General Foote was a featured speaker at the Democratic National Convention in Chicago.

Alfred P. Galvan, Lombard, Illinois
Alfred Galvan has served 12 terms as State Chairman of the Illinois American G.I. Forum. He has devoted his adult life to upgrading the educational and socio-economic opportunities for all minority groups, which particular emphasis on the needs of Hispanics. He served for three years in the U.S. Army.

Senator James Henderson, Window Rock, Arizona
Senator James Henderson has served in the Arizona State Senate since 1985. He served in the U.S. Army from 1966 until 1968 in Vietnam, where he received a Purple Heart. He is a member of the Navajo Tribe.

Lt. Gen. Thomas W. Kelly (ret.), Clifton, Virginia
Lt. Gen. Kelly was the Director of Operations (J3) for the Joint Chiefs of Staff and played a crucial role in the planning and execution of military operations during the Persian Gulf War. Many remember Gen. Kelly for his daily televised press briefings from the Pentagon during the Persian Gulf War. His awards include the Defense Distinguished Service Medal, the Army Distinguished Service Medal, three awards of the Legion of Merit, the Distinguished Flying Cross, five Bronze Stars, and the Purple Heart.

Billy Kirby, Zebulon, North Carolina
Billy Kirby was the National Commander of Disabled American Veterans in 1988-89. He served in the U.S. Army from 1940 until 1946, receiving a Purple Heart for his injuries in Italy in 1944. He also worked for Congressman Olin Teague on the Committee on Veterans Affairs in the 1960s and 1970s.

Edward J. Lucyk, Mahanoy City, Pennsylvania,
Edward Lucyk is in his eighth term in the Pennsylvania House of Representatives, where is the Democratic Chairman of the Veterans Affairs and Emergency Preparedness Committee. He served in the U.S. Army for eight years, including two tours of duty in Vietnam, and retired with the rank of captain. He was awarded the bronze star medal with three oak leaf clusters.

Richard Marbes, Green Bay, Wisconsin
Richard Marbes served as National Commander of the Disabled American Veterans in 1993-94. He joined the U.S. Air Force in 1955 and served in Germany until he retired in 1958 when a service-related injury forced the amputation of his right leg. Wisconsin Governor Tommy Thompson appointed him to a four-year term on the State Board of Veterans Affairs.

Mayor Jim Marshall, Macon, Georgia
Jim Marshall was elected mayor of Macon, Georgia, in November 1995 with 68 percent of the vote. He dropped out of Princeton University in 1968 to volunteer for the war in Vietnam, where he served in the Army and was awarded two Bronze Stars, the Combat Infantry Badge, and the Purple Heart.

Gail M. Reals, Arlington, Virginia
Gail Reals served in the U.S. Marine Corps from 1954 until 1990, where she worked her way up from a private to brigadier general, becoming the first woman general in the Marine Corps. Her medals include the Legion of Merit and the Distinguished Service Medal. She has since received her B.A. from George Mason University in the Development of Public Policy.

Lewis Roberts, Lakeland, Florida
Lewis Roberts reached the rank of Captain in the Marine Corps. He is "Mr. Veteran" in the state of Florida and a strong supporter of President Clinton.

Admiral Elmo Russell Zumwalt, Jr. (Ret.), Arlington, Virginia
Admiral Zumwalt was Chief of Naval Operations and a member of the Joint Chiefs of Staff from 1970 to 1974. He was the recipient of the Distinguished Service Medal with two Gold Stars, the Legion of Merit with one Gold Star, Bronze Star with Combat V. He and his late son, Elmo Zumwalt III, co-authored My Father, My Son, which described their battle to deal with the son�s Agent Orange-induced cancer.
