WHY JEWISH VOTERS SHOULD RE-ELECT PRESIDENT CLINTON

Washington, D.C. -- On election day, November 5, Jewish voters will play a significant role in deciding the course of the last election of the twentieth century. In deciding whom Jewish voters should support, the choice is clear. President Clinton has a proven record of accomplishments on issues of concern to the Jewish community. In 1992, when nearly 85% of Jewish voters chose Clinton/Gore, President Clinton came into office with a plan -- a plan that continues to work. American now have more jobs and more opportunity, less crime and stronger communities.

"As we come closer to the 21st Century we need leaders with imagination in social justice, leadership with moral inspiration... This year, one must take sides and say whether we consider President Clinton and Vice President Gore worthy of our trust, and I believe they are," said Clinton/Gore �96 Jewish Leadership Council Honorary Chair Elie Wiesel on a national conference call with First Lady Hillary Rodham Clinton.

"During his first term, President Clinton has not backed down from tackling difficult issues. President Clinton stood up to the National Rifle Association..been strong on the issue of affirmative action... [and] demonstrated a strong commitment to providing educational opportunities for young people," said David Hermelin, Honorary Co-Chair of the Jewish Leadership Council.

Honorary Co-Chair Lynn Lyss added, "President Clinton has done his best to ensure that women�s voices are heard at every level of government...has appointed more women to his administration than any other president in history...[and] has appointed a record number of women to the federal bench."

"[D]uring the last four years, ...[Clinton] has, because of his unwavering commitment to strengthening the US-Israel relationship, enhanced the security of Israel and its people as well as the cause of world peace. Simply put, Israel has never had a better friend in the White House...and I have no doubt that he will continue to provide this crucial support in his second term," asserted Honorary Vice Chair Steve Grossman.

"This election is the most important that I have experienced in my long career in public service. As an elected official, I am intimately familiar with the excellent work done by the President and the critical role the Jewish voter will play in re-electing the Clinton/Gore team," concluded Honorary Co-Chair Sheldon Silver, who is Speaker of the New York Assembly.
