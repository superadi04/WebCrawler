AGRICULTURE LEADERS FROM 18 STATES ENDORSE
RE-ELECTION OF PRESIDENT CLINTON AND VICE PRESIDENT GORE

Agriculture officials from 18 states today announced their full support for the re-election of President Clinton and Vice President Gore at a news conference at the National Press Club in Washington, D.C.

"We are thrilled to have the endorsement of prominent leaders in the agriculture community," said Peter S. Knight, Clinton/Gore �96 Campaign Manager. "This constitutes a strong validation of President Clinton�s sensible approach to agriculture."

The officials pointed to Clinton Administration accomplishments and the President�s sound approach to rural issues as the reason for their endorsement.

"The Administration said they would make life better for farmers, and they have," said Bob Odom who is the Louisiana Agriculture Commissioner. "Income is higher, prices are better, exports are growing, and rural home ownership is up. In every case," added Odom, "the President has listened to our concerns and helped implement new approaches to solving the problems of rural America."

Agriculture officials from Delaware, Florida, Georgia, Hawaii, Indiana, Iowa, Kentucky, Louisiana, Maryland, Mississippi, Missouri, New Hampshire, North Carolina, North Dakota, Oregon, Vermont, Washington, West Virginia pledged their support for the President.

"One of the key accomplishments during President Clinton�s first term was the restructuring of the U.S. Department of Agriculture," said Jim Graham who is the North Carolina Agriculture Commissioner. The USDA has led federal reinvention with the most massive restructuring in our history. The Clinton Administration�s plan for reorganizing USDA has already saved taxpayers over $900 million and cut staff by 10,000 positions. Added Bruce Andrews who is the Oregon Commissioner, "The new farm bill represents a historic, much needed change in the way U.S. agriculture competes at home and in the international marketplace."
