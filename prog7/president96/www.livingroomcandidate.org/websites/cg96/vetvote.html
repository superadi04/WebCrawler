VETERANS FACE IMPORTANT CHOICE ON ELECTION DAY

WASHINGTON, D.C. -- On election day, November 5, veterans and their families will play a key role in deciding the course of America�s future. Their choice is clear. President Clinton has a proven record of accomplishments for veterans. American veterans have better VA health care, more jobs and opportunities, and stronger communities than four years ago.

President Clinton expanded veterans� benefits. President Clinton expanded disability compensation for Vietnam veterans exposed to Agent Orange and their children and for Persian Gulf veterans with undiagnosed illnesses. He fought for full cost-of-living adjustments on benefits for disabled veterans and military retirees. In contrast, in 1995 Dole voted to eliminate compensation for certain incompetent service-disabled veterans, to cut veterans� medical care by $511 million, and to kill two new VA hospitals.

President Clinton re-vamped VA health care and protected it from Republican budget cuts. As part of his Reinventing Government initiative,

President Clinton reformed VA health care, cutting red tape and improving patient care. He also fought for $1 billion more in fiscal 1996 to care for more veterans and build new hospitals.

President Clinton promoted economic opportunities for veterans. Under President Clinton�s leadership, the federal government has increased the percentage of new federal jobs filled by veterans by 10 points and helped over 1.5 million veterans find civilian jobs. The unemployment rate among veterans has dropped by more than one-third.

President Clinton expanded America�s economy. In 1993, without a single Republican vote, President Clinton pushed through his deficit reduction package. Since then, more than 10.5 million new jobs have been created; the deficit has been cut by 63%; the average household income has increased by $1,600; and household income inequality fell more in 1995 than in any year since 1968.

President Clinton strengthened our communities. President Clinton is making our streets safer by adding 100,000 new cops on the beat, taking guns away from criminals through the Brady Bill, and banning 19 types of assault weapons. His approach is working: the violent crime rate has fallen four straight years.

"For veterans, the choice is clear: President Clinton is on their side," said

Tom Keefe, Director of Veterans Outreach. "His sterling record on bread-and-butter veterans� issues explains why he was endorsed by veterans nationwide, including Medal of Honor recipients, past national commanders of major veterans organizations, and other prominent veterans including Jesse Brown, who is the Secretary of Veterans Affairs, Hershel Gober, who is the Deputy Secretary of Veterans Affairs, Lieutenant General (ret) Thomas Kelly, and Admiral (ret) Elmo Zumwalt."
