PRESIDENT CLINTON REAFFIRMS LONGSTANDING COMMITMENT TO QUALITY HEALTH CARE

President Clinton has reaffirmed his longstanding commitment to quality health care. Recognizing that pressures on America�s health plans and health providers at times could have unintended effects on quality of care, President Clinton announced initiatives that would provide important patient protections. President Clinton�s speech in Sunrise, Florida announced the following initiatives:

President Clinton endorsed bipartisan legislation that would prohibit health plans from restricting medical communications between health care providers and their patients. President Clinton also announced his support for the extension of this "anti-gag rule" to Medicare plans.

President Clinton reiterated his support for bipartisan legislation that would require health plans to pay for 48 hours of post-partum care for new mothers and their babies. In their speeches during the Democratic National Convention, both President Clinton and First Lady Hillary Rodham Clinton endorsed this "48-hour rule."

President Clinton signed an executive order to create the Advisory Commission on Consumer Protection and Quality in the Health Care Industry. This commission will recommend public and private mechanisms to ensure quality health care in America�s evolving health care delivery systems. It will be co-chaired by the Secretary of Health and Human Services and the Secretary of Labor, and its final report will be reviewed by the Vice President before submission to the President. Its membership will include health care consumers, providers, insurers, and purchasers, and other experts in quality health care.

"These policies will go a long way towards protecting health care consumers and will continue moving health care policy for all Americans into the 21st century," said Steve Gleason, the Campaign�s Senior Health Policy Advisor.
