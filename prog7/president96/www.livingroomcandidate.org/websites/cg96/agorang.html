PRESIDENT CLINTON SUPPORTS THE FAMILIES OF
VIETNAM VETERANS EXPOSED TO AGENT ORANGE

In a ceremony at the White House this week, President Clinton signed legislation to provide relief to the children of Vietnam veterans exposed to the defoliant Agent Orange.

"I am very, very proud that this legislation will protect the families of Vietnam veterans who were exposed to Agent Orange," President Clinton said in his Rose Garden remarks. "Far too many of these brave soldiers have suffered illness as a result of their service, and now we know that, for many of them, the health effects were also passed along to their children. That is more than anyone can ask in terms of service to the nation."

The VA-HUD Appropriations Act includes provisions to provide a monthly allowance, lifetime health care benefits, and vocational training for the children of Vietnam veterans born with spina bifida. President Clinton proposed the legislation to Congress last August.

The bill was supported by a wide range of veterans� groups, including the Vietnam Veterans of America, the Veterans of Foreign Wars, the American Legion, AMVETS, and the Disabled American Veterans.
