Washington, D.C. -- The Clinton/Gore �96 campaign today announced the creation of the "Clinton/Gore�96 Lesbian and Gay Leadership Council," an organization that will reach out to gay and lesbian Americans to support the reelection of President Clinton and Vice President Gore.

Members of the Council are comprised of key supporters and community leaders from all 50 states and the District of Columbia. They also serve as Honorary State Co-Chairs. The Council will organize state committees that will coordinate lesbian and gay get-out-the-vote activities in support of the Clinton/Gore ticket.

"The strength of this campaign lies in its inclusiveness," said Paul Yandura, National Director of Lesbian and Gay Outreach for Clinton/Gore �96, "The efforts of this council to get out the vote will make an important difference in this election."

The two Honorary National Co-chairs for the Council are Roberta Achtenberg of California and Jeff Soref of New York. Achtenberg became the first openly lesbian or gay person confirmed by the U.S. Senate, when President Clinton nominated her as Assistant Secretary of Housing and Urban Development. Soref is Co-Chair of the Empire State Pride Agenda, New York State�s gay and lesbian political organization and had previously served as Co-Chair of Gay Men�s Health Crisis.
